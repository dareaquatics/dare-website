General Information

What is club swimming?
Club swimming is a year-round competitive program governed by USA Swimming. Unlike recreational lessons, it focuses on technical mastery, endurance, and competitive racing in a team environment.
What is DARE Aquatics' mission?
Our mission is to offer age-specific training that challenges and develops each individual to the best of their abilities, fostering a lifelong love for the sport and personal growth.
What age range do you serve?
We generally serve swimmers from age 5 through 18. We have groups ranging from the beginner "DARE Devils" to our "National Group" for college-bound athletes.
Where are the pools located?
We primarily practice at Alhambra Park Pool and Granada Pool. Locations may vary by group and season.
What is "Southern California Swimming" (SCS)?
SCS is the regional governing body for USA Swimming in our area. They coordinate the meet schedule and time standards for all clubs in Southern California.
What is the "Metro Committee"?
We are part of the Metro Committee within Southern California Swimming. This is the local subdivision that hosts many of our seasonal championship meets.
Can my high schooler join?
Absolutely. We have many high school athletes. During the CIF season, swimmers may "unattach" to represent their schools while continuing to train with us.
Do you have a "Masters" program for adults?
Currently, DARE focuses on youth age-group and senior swimming. We do not have a dedicated Masters program at this time.
How many swimmers are on the team?
The team size fluctuates, but we typically have over 100 active swimmers across all our training sites and groups.
What is the difference between DARE and the Alhambra Dolphins?
The Alhambra Dolphins are often a city-run novice program. DARE Aquatics is an independent USA Swimming year-round club focusing on higher-level competitive development.
Does swimming help with college admissions?
Yes! Competitive swimming demonstrates discipline and time management. Our National Group specifically prepares swimmers for the rigors of collegiate swimming.
Are there team social events?
Yes, we host holiday parties, year-end banquets, and occasional team-building activities, such as beach outings"
What happens during pool renovations?
If a primary pool is closed, we utilize our other locations to ensure training continues without interruption.
Is the club open during holidays?
We typically have a short break in late August and during major holidays (Christmas/New Year). Please check the calendar on Facebook for specific dates.
Do you offer one-on-one lessons?
While our focus is group training, some coaches may be available for private stroke refinement outside of regular practice hours for a fee.
Can I join if I'm not from Alhambra?
Yes! We have swimmers from all over the San Gabriel Valley and East Los Angeles areas.
What is the commitment level?
It varies by group. DARE Devils may practice 2-3 times a week, while National Group athletes are expected to attend 6 days a week plus dryland.
How do I contact the board?
You can use our contact form to reach our administration and board members for any official inquiries.

Registration and Membership

How do I schedule a tryout?
The best way is to use our website's Tryout Request form.
What should I bring to a tryout?
A swimsuit, goggles, and a towel. If you have long hair, please bring a swim cap. Coaches will evaluate the swimmer's comfort and stroke ability in the water.
What is the GoMotion (TeamUnify) account?
GoMotion (TeamUnify) is our member portal. It is used for all registrations, meet sign-ups, and monthly billing. You must have an active account to participate.
Is USA Swimming membership mandatory?
Yes. All athletes must hold a current USA Swimming membership for insurance purposes and to be eligible for competition. This is an annual fee paid through the USA Swimming portal.
When is the monthly billing cycle?
Dues are typically billed on the 1st of each month via GoMotion (TeamUnify). Auto-pay is highly recommended.
What happens if I pay late?
A late fee may be assessed if dues are not paid by the 5th of the month. Chronic late payments may result in the swimmer being suspended from practice.
How do I cancel my membership?
We require written notice (usually 15-30 days) via email or contact form. Simply stopping attendance does not cancel billing.
Can I pause my membership for vacation?
Membership is year-round. We do not typically "pause" for short vacations, but you can discuss extended medical leaves with the head coach.
What is "Safe Sport" training for parents?
Safe Sport is a program to prevent abuse in sports. We encourage all parents to take the free online training available through USA Swimming.
What is the 60-day rule for transfers?
If you transfer from another USA Swimming club, you must wait 60 days from your last competition with your previous club before you can represent DARE in a meet.
What does "Unattached" (UN) status mean?
Swimmers who have recently transferred or are competing for a high school team swim as "Unattached." They can still win awards but cannot score points for the team.
Do you offer financial aid?
We strive to keep swimming accessible. Please contact our head coach to discuss financial assistance options if you are experiencing hardship.
How often can my child be promoted?
Promotions happen based on skill mastery, age, and coach recommendation. There is no set schedule; it depends on individual progress.
Is there an annual club registration fee?
Yes, there is a yearly DARE club fee (separate from USA Swimming) that covers equipment, insurance, and administrative costs.
Can I update my credit card on the app?
Yes, you can manage all billing info through the GoMotion (TeamUnify) mobile app or the desktop website.
What medical forms are required?
During registration on GoMotion (TeamUnify), you will sign electronic waivers and provide emergency contact and medical information.
Is the tryout fee refundable?
Tryouts are typically free of charge! We want to make sure DARE is the right fit for your family first.
Do I need to provide a birth certificate?
Yes, USA Swimming requires age verification. You will usually upload a copy during the registration process.
What if my child is 18?
Athletes 18 and older must complete APT (Athlete Protection Training) to remain eligible for USA Swimming competition.

Training and Practice

What are the DARE Devils?
DARE Devils is our entry-level group for water-safe beginners. They focus on freestyle, backstroke, and learning the fundamentals of a team practice.
What is "Dryland" training?
Dryland includes strength and conditioning exercises done on land to improve core strength, flexibility, and power in the water.
What is "Circle Swimming"?
In a crowded lane, swimmers swim on the right side of the lane (counter-clockwise) to avoid head-on collisions. This is standard in club swimming.
Should my child eat before practice?
A light, carb-based snack 1-2 hours before practice is ideal. Avoid heavy meals right before jumping in.
What equipment is required for Age Group Elite?
Elite swimmers typically need fins, a kickboard, a pull buoy, hand paddles, and a snorkel. Specific lists are provided by Coach Joy.
What is the attendance policy?
Higher-level groups have strict attendance requirements (e.g., 90%+) to stay in the group. Younger groups are more flexible, though consistency is key for progress.
What if we are late to practice?
If you are late, the swimmer should wait for the coach to signal them into the lane. Repeated lateness may result in the swimmer being asked to sit out.
Are pools heated?
Yes, the city pools we use are heated to competitive standards (usually between 78 and 82 degrees Fahrenheit).
What is a "Set" and an "Interval"?
A "Set" is a series of distances (e.g., 10 x 50 yards). An "Interval" is the total time allowed for the swim plus the rest.
Can I watch from the pool deck?
For safety and focus, parents are asked to stay out of the pool deck and to not approach it during practice.
How do I handle an injury?
Notify your group coach immediately. We can often modify workouts (e.g., "kick only") to allow the swimmer to stay in shape while recovering.
Do you have morning (AM) practices?
Yes, for senior-level groups and during the summer, we often have early morning sessions to fit in double practices.
What if it's cold outside?
The water is heated, but we recommend swimmers have a warm parka or heavy robe and a beanie to wear as soon as they exit the water.
Can my child swim with contacts or braces?
Yes. Goggles protect contacts well. For braces, we recommend a orthodontic mouthguard if the swimmer is doing high-intensity starts or turns.
How long are the practices?
Sessions range from 1 hour for DARE Devils to 2+ hours for the National and Elite groups.
What is "Tapering"?
Tapering is a reduction in training volume before a big meet. It allows the body to recover and perform at peak speed.
Is swimming every day too much?
Swimming is a low-impact sport. For competitive athletes, daily training is necessary for cardiovascular and muscle memory development.
What is "IM"?
IM stands for Individual Medley, which is one race involving all four strokes: Butterfly, Backstroke, Breaststroke, and Freestyle (in that order).
Do you offer weekend practices?
Yes, we typically have Saturday morning practices at locations like Alhambra or Granada. Check the calendar in the Facebook group.
Why do swimmers wear caps?
Caps reduce drag, keep hair out of the eyes/mouth, and help protect the pool filtration system from loose hair.

Competitions

Where do I find the meet schedule?
The full schedule is posted on the "Events" tab of our website and within the GoMotion (TeamUnify) app. Alternatively, you can also check the Calendar page.
How do I sign up for a meet?
Log into GoMotion (TeamUnify), find the event, and click "Attend/Decline." You can then select the events your swimmer wishes to enter.
What are "Metro Champs"?
This is our local championship meet held twice a year (Short Course and Long Course). It is often mandatory for DARE swimmers.
What is "Short Course" vs "Long Course"?
Short Course (SCY) is 25-yard pools, swum in the fall/winter. Long Course (LCM) is 50-meter pools, swum in the spring/summer.
What is a "DQ"?
A Disqualification occurs when a swimmer breaks a stroke rule. Officials will explain the DQ to the swimmer so they can learn for next time.
What is "Meet Mobile"?
Meet Mobile is a popular 3rd-party app (not GoMotion) used to track live results, heat sheets, and times during a swim meet.
Do I need a tech suit?
USA Swimming prohibits high-end tech suits for 12 & under swimmers. For older swimmers, they are optional for "end of season" championship meets.
What are "Ribbons" and "Medals"?
Awards are typically given for 1st-8th place in various age categories. These are often distributed to coaches after the meet and handed out at practice.
What is a "Heat Sheet"?
A program that lists every swimmer's event, heat number, and lane. You should always mark your swimmer's hand with their "E-H-L" (Event-Heat-Lane).
How do relays work?
Relays consist of 4 swimmers. Coaches select relay teams based on current times to maximize the team's competitive edge.
What does "NT" mean?
NT stands for "No Time." It means the swimmer has never officially competed in that event before.
What is a "Psych Sheet"?
A list of all swimmers entered in an event, ranked from fastest to slowest. It helps you see where you stand before the meet starts.
When should we arrive for a meet?
Usually 15-30 minutes before "Warm-ups" begin. Warm-ups often start at 7:00 AM or 8:00 AM for morning sessions.
What is "Checking In"?
Some meets require swimmers to "check in" on a sheet to confirm they are present. If you don't check in, you will be "scratched" (removed) from the event.
Are meet entry fees refundable?
No. Once the meet entry deadline passes and the files are sent to the host team, fees are non-refundable even if the swimmer is sick.
Can I enter any event I want?
You can suggest events in GoMotion (TeamUnify), but coaches have final approval to ensure the swimmer is entered in appropriate distances for their skill level.
What is an "Invitational"?
An invitational is a larger meet where specific clubs are invited. They often have stricter "qualifying times" (standardized time cuts).
What should we pack for a meet?
Extra towels, two pairs of goggles, snacks, water, a sharpie (for E-H-L), and chairs if the facility doesn't have bleachers.
What is "Warm-down"?
After a race, swimmers swim slowly for a few hundred yards to flush lactic acid from their muscles. This is vital for multi-day meets.
Do I have to stay for the whole meet?
You can leave once your events and potential relays are finished, but we encourage staying to cheer on teammates!

Coaching and Support

Who is the Head Coach?
Our coaching staff is led by experienced professionals like Coach Joseph, Coach Sam, and Coach Joy, each overseeing different age and skill divisions. Our head coach is Coach Joy.
Are coaches certified?
Yes, all DARE coaches are USA Swimming certified, which includes background checks, CPR/First Aid training, and safety-on-deck certifications.
How do I get feedback on my child's progress?
Coaches provide feedback during practice. If you need a more detailed discussion, please contact the coach to set up a meeting outside of practice time.
What is the coach-to-swimmer ratio?
We aim to keep ratios that allow for individual attention, typically ranging from 1:10 for beginners to 1:20 for older, self-sufficient athletes.
Can coaches help with college recruiting?
Yes, our senior-level coaches (Coach Joy) are very experienced in helping athletes navigate the NCAA recruiting process and contact college coaches.
Do coaches travel to all meets?
DARE ensures that at least one coach is on deck for all "sanctioned" meets listed on our official calendar.
Why didn't the coach talk to my child after a DQ?
Coaches usually wait for the swimmer to finish their warm-down before discussing race technicalities. They will always try to provide constructive feedback.
Can I request a specific lane for my child?
No. Lane assignments are determined by the coaches based on speed, safety, and the technical focus of the set.
What is the philosophy on "Winning"?
We value "Personal Bests" over medals. If a swimmer improves their time, they have succeeded, regardless of their place in the heat.
Are there substitute coaches?
Occasionally, coaches may cover for one another. All DARE coaches follow the same technical curriculum to ensure consistency.
How are coaches background checked?
Every coach undergoes a rigorous federal background check through USA Swimming every two years.
Do coaches offer nutritional advice?
We provide general guidance on hydration and competition fueling. For specific dietary needs, we recommend consulting a sports nutritionist.
Can a coach move my child down a group?
Rarely, but if a swimmer is struggling with the intensity or has missed significant time, a coach may suggest a lower group to rebuild their foundation.
What if I have a conflict with a coach?
We encourage open communication. Please first try to resolve it with the coach directly; if needed, you may contact the Head Coach or Board.
Do coaches teach sportsmanship?
Yes. DARE swimmers are expected to shake hands with competitors and cheer for their teammates. Respect is a core value.
How do coaches handle bullying?
We have a zero-tolerance policy. Bullying results in immediate suspension and a meeting with the parents and board.
Can I pay for extra coaching?
You can inquire about private lessons, but group practices are designed to provide all the training a competitive swimmer needs.
Do coaches give homework?
Occasionally, older swimmers are given stretching or dryland routines to do at home to maintain flexibility.
What is a coach's role at a meet?
To organize warm-ups, provide race strategy, and analyze performance. They are not there to "babysit"; parents are responsible for swimmers between races.

Additional Information

Where can I buy team gear?
Team suits, caps, and Spirit Wear are typically sold through our online "Team Store" or during organized fittings. Check GoMotion (TeamUnify) for links.
What is the "Service Points" system?
To keep the club running, parents earn points by volunteering (timing at meets, helping at events). A minimum number of points is required per family.
How do I become a swim official?
SCS offers clinics for parents to become Stroke & Turn judges or Starters. This is a great way to support the team and see the races up close!
Is there a "Lost and Found"?
Items left at Alhambra Park or Granada pools are usually kept in the lifeguard office. Please label all your gear with a permanent marker!
Can I sponsor the team?
Yes! We have several sponsorship tiers for local businesses. Visit our Sponsor Us page for more information.
How do I get my swimmer's times?
All official times are recorded in the USA Swimming "SWIMS" database and are easily viewable in your GoMotion (TeamUnify) profile.
What is the "Clothing Drive"?
We occasionally host clothing drives to raise funds for the team. Parents can earn service points by helping weigh and load donation bags.
Do you have a newsletter?
Yes, important updates are sent via the GoMotion (TeamUnify) email system. Please ensure your email is marked as "verified" in your account.
Can I volunteer if I don't know how to swim?
Yes! Many roles (timing, hospitality, registration) do not require swimming knowledge. We will train you on how to use a stopwatch!
What is the "Status Page"?
Our Status Page provides real-time information if our website or member portals are experiencing technical issues.
Are there "travel meets"?
Yes, older groups occasionally travel to meets in San Diego, Nevada, or even Arizona for regional competitions.
What is the photo policy?
We often take photos at meets and practices for social media. You can opt-out of this by notifying us in writing during registration.
Is the team on social media?
Yes! Follow us on Instagram and join our private Facebook Group.
Where can I find the team bylaws?
Our official bylaws and team policy documents are available on the Team Policy page.
What if I move away?
Please notify us 30 days in advance. We can provide your swimmer's full time history to help them transition to a new club.
Does DARE host its own meets?
Yes, we occasionally host "Intrasquad" meets or local invitationals. These are primary events for earning parent service points.
How do I contact the webmaster?
If you find an issue with the site, use the contact form and mention "Website Issue" in the subject line.
What is the year-end banquet?
A celebration of the year's achievements, featuring food, trophies, and a slideshow of the "DARE Bunch" in action.
Why are email addresses hidden?
To prevent automated spam bots from scraping our coaches' contact info. Just replace '[at]' with '@'.
